Statistical Arbitrage Portfolio

This project uses Markowitz optimization to trade a portfolio of mean-reverting stock pairs, aiming to outperform the S&P 500.

Key Findings

  • By using cointegration tests, volatility filters, and z-score thresholds, the model successfully narrowed down thousands of potential stock pairs to a diversified portfolio of 50 high-quality pairs.
  • The model especially outperformed the S&P 500 in bear markets by exploiting more frequent price discrepancies from heightened volatility and mispricing.
  • The trading model delivered cumulative profits similar to the S&P 500, but its reliance on cointegration, a long-term concept, made it less effective at interpreting short-term trends.
